September 7-9, 2018 @ JHU Medical Campus

MedHacks, the world’s premier medical hackathon, is proud to announce its fourth annual hackathon, MedHacks 2018: Snow Day! Join us from September 7-9 at the Johns Hopkins Hospital for 36 hours of innovation at crossroads of medical entrepreneurship and technological development.

About

Our History

MedHacks was born in 2014 as a result of seeing the extreme disparity between the areas in medicine that need innovation and the growing interest in entrepreneurship in our generation. The idea behind MedHacks is that you don’t need an MD or PHD to hack health, nor do you need a background in computer science. All you need is an interest in making the world a better place.

The MedHacks Factor

MedHacks doesn’t care about whether or not you’ve won coding competitions, or even if you’re pursuing an engineering or CS degree. In fact, one of our founding principles is that you don’t need a PHD or MD to innovate in healthcare, all you need is an interest in saving the world. That’s exemplified in our a typical participant demographics: last year at MedHacks 2017, we had over 750 hackers for over 300 schools and 15 countries, with diverse majors ranging from public health to philosophy to computer science. We even had a number of professionals (including a surgeon from India) participate!

Introducing MedHacks 2018: Snow Day

We’re thrilled to introduce the theme for MedHacks 2018: Snow Day! It’s been a long, exhausting year, and we know that we could all use a day (or three) to relax, not think about school work, huddle up with a computer and some teammates, and just focus on innovating some healthcare. Even though September in Baltimore is not exactly known for its below-zero temperatures, we’ll be sure to bring the cool within the Johns Hopkins Hospital with our trusty moose, Melvin, by our sides!

Tracks

At MedHacks 2018: Frostbitten, you’ll have the choice between three sponsored tracks. The hack you decide to do must fall within the track that you choose and you will be judged within your track for a track-specific prize. Tracks from previous years have been Medication Adherence with CVS Health and Access to Care with Blue Cross Blue Shield.

Stay tuned for an announcement of tracks for MedHacks 2018!

How do I apply?

Applications for MedHacks 2018 are not open yet, but sign into our application portal here.

How can I tell the status of my application or travel reimbursement application?

Sign into the MedHacks application portal here, and your application status, as well as the status of any other pending applications (travel reimbursements, etc), will be displayed on your profile page.

Where is MedHacks 2018 Located?

MedHacks 2018 is located at the Turner Auditorium in the Ross Research Building at the Johns Hopkins Medical Campus in East Baltimore, MD. The Ross Research Building is located at 720 Rutland Avenue, Baltimore, MD 21218. If you are traveling from the Homewood Campus, you can take the JHMI or special MedHacks buses to the event. If you are driving to the event, there will be parking on campus and more details will be emailed out closer to the event. If you are coming from the airport, train station, or hotel, we highly recommend you take a cab/lyft to the building. The medical campus is in the city, so please be safe when traveling to the event.

Who can participate in MedHacks 2018?

Anyone is eligible to participate in MedHacks 2018, however if you’re under 18, we ask that a parent or guardian be in the Baltimore area at all times during the event.

If you’re a graduate student or professional, you can choose to join MedHacks either as a participant or as a mentor!

I’m not an engineer, but I’m interested in participating. Is that ok?

That’s more than OK! In fact, that’s encouraged! We love introducing people from all kinds of backgrounds to the world of innovation.

How can I get a travel reimbursement?

If you are accepted and remarked on your application that you would need a travel reimbursement, you may be invited to apply for a travel reimbursement. In that case, sign into the MedHacks application portal and follow the links for the travel reimbursement application.

Once you are accepted for a reimbursement, you must submit a VALID receipt. Guidelines for valid receipts are here. Your reimbursement is contingent upon your submitting a valid receipt. Failure to do so will result in MedHacks’ inability to issue said reimbursement.

I don’t have a team or an idea. What should I do?

That’s perfectly alright! In fact, we don’t allow participants to enter with an idea they’ve been working on for a while and encourage teams to be formed during one of MedHacks’ ideation sessions.

Apply

MedHacks receives thousands of applications for fantastic people all around the world every year, but unfortunately we don’t have the space to accommodate everyone who applies. We follow a rolling application basis, so the earlier you apply, the more likely you’ll be able to have a decision! Additionally, if you’re interested in applying for a travel reimbursement, you must apply to MedHacks by July 15.

MedHacks prides itself on being accessible for all students, especially those without any kind of computing background, and so we try as hard as possible to reflect that in the population of participants at the event. We encourage all individuals remotely interested in the fields of medicine and innovation to apply, regardless of skill level. We’d love to have you!

MedHacks is empowering young engineers to delve into the med-tech field and create new products that will revolutionize the field of medicine.
